# Content Safety

Last updated: 2026-04-29

This file defines the safety bar for public content on `careleaver.eu`.

## Core Safety Principle

The site should help people prepare, find support, and ask better questions. It should not act like a lawyer, doctor, therapist, emergency dispatcher, benefits office, or case decision-maker.

## High-Risk Content Areas

Treat these areas as high-risk:

- emergency and crisis instructions
- self-harm, violence, abuse, homelessness, or acute danger
- mental health and medical information
- legal rights, legal deadlines, appeals, and entitlement language
- benefits, housing, money, debt, and official applications
- child/youth welfare, aftercare, and case-plan decisions
- personal data, identity documents, and sensitive appointment preparation

High-risk content must be source-linked, dated, and written cautiously.

## Crisis And Emergency Content

- Keep emergency numbers easy to find.
- Use official emergency/crisis sources.
- Do not bury urgent routes behind long explanations.
- Do not imply the website can handle emergencies.
- For immediate danger, direct users to emergency services or local crisis services.
- Check crisis numbers regularly.

## Legal / Benefits / Housing Content

- Do not promise outcomes.
- Do not say a person definitely qualifies unless all conditions are clear and sourced.
- Prefer "check whether", "ask", "bring these documents", and "contact the official office".
- Make source links visible near the relevant claim.
- Include the date or "last checked" where possible.

## Health And Mental Health Content

- Do not diagnose.
- Do not recommend medication or clinical treatment plans.
- Do not replace professional help.
- For mental health crisis content, route to official crisis services and emergency numbers.

## Language Style

Use calm, practical German.

Good style:

- short sentences
- clear next steps
- respectful wording
- no shame, no blame
- no bureaucratic overconfidence

Avoid:

- scary or dramatic wording
- fake certainty
- legalistic walls of text
- implying the young person caused the problem
- making support feel conditional on being "perfect" or "organized"

## Privacy And Data

- Avoid collecting personal data.
- Do not add server-side forms without an explicit privacy decision.
- If browser local storage is used, disclose it clearly.
- Do not ask users to enter highly sensitive details unless there is a clear local-only reason.

## Images And People

If images are added later:

- avoid exploiting distress
- avoid identifiable vulnerable young people unless there is explicit consent and a strong reason
- prefer practical interface clarity over emotional stock imagery

## When Unsure

Use the safer wording:

> "This can depend on your situation. Check the current official information and ask a trusted support person or the responsible office."

Then add the uncertainty to `research/open-questions.md`.
