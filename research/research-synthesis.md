# Research Synthesis

Last updated: 2026-04-29

This synthesis summarizes the research swarm run on 2026-04-29. It is a product and content-direction pass, not a public content update. No big site-content changes have been published yet.

## Single Best MVP Direction

Make `careleaver.eu` a **Wien-first, source-dated "Was mache ich als Naechstes?" guide for Care Leavers after child/youth welfare**.

In practical terms, the site should become a low-maintenance static companion for:

- quick crisis and safety routing
- preparing for MA 11 / Care Leaver counselling
- housing and first-flat next steps
- income, U25, AMS, benefits, and debt-prep questions
- health, mental-health, relationship, and peer-support routing
- documents, appointments, and scripts for difficult calls

The site should **not** become a legal encyclopedia, benefit calculator, advocacy organization, chatbot, or broad contact database. Its highest-impact role is helping a young person or helper get to the right official or trusted service with better questions and better paperwork.

## Why This Direction

Care Leavers commonly face the expectation of early independence with weaker family, money, housing, and fallback support than many peers. Austrian and European sources repeatedly point to housing, work/education, finances, health, mental health, social isolation, documents, authorities, and stigma as transition problems.

The current page already has useful ingredients: crisis bar, checklist, timeline, rights cards, tools, Wien contacts, Bundesland placeholders, and a one-page plan. The research shows the main problem is not missing ambition. The main problem is **source confidence, safe wording, and practical routing**.

## Need Discovery

Core needs to support first:

| Need area | What research suggests | Site implication |
|---|---|---|
| Crisis and safety | Immediate danger, mental-health crisis, violence, homelessness, and acute youth distress need clear official routes. | Put "Schnelle Hilfe in Wien" first. Separate immediate danger, mental-health crisis, youth advice, and housing emergency. |
| Housing | Wohnungssuche, first flat, rent, deposit, Meldezettel, Wohnbeihilfe, eviction risk, and homelessness are central transition issues. | Make housing the first non-crisis practical journey. Use checklists and "ask before signing" prompts. |
| Money and income | Benefits, AMS/U25, Mindestsicherung, debt, bank account, ORF/fees, and unstable income are high-risk topics. | Provide appointment prep and official links. Avoid promising eligibility or outcomes. |
| Health and mental health | Care Leavers can face reduced health equity, psychosocial stress, social isolation, and crisis risk. | Route to real services; use calm, non-diagnostic wording; avoid therapy/medical advice. |
| Education and work | Transition from school to work/training is a repeated challenge, and stigma can affect education pathways. | Use supportive language and link to U25, NEBA, AusBildung bis 18, waff/AMS routes after source checks. |
| Documents and authorities | Meldezettel, ID, insurance, forms, appointment notes, decisions, and evidence are practical bottlenecks. | Build printable appointment kits: what to bring, what to ask, what to write down. |
| Relationships and support | Stable adults, peer support, mentors, former carers, and self-representation matter. | Link prominently to Care Leaver Oesterreich and official Wien Care Leaver counselling; avoid replacing peer/community work. |
| Stigma and shame | Deficit framing can make support feel conditional or humiliating. | Use rights-based, plain German: "Du darfst fragen", "Du kannst jemanden mitnehmen", "Bitte schriftlich erklaeren lassen." |

## Austria / Wien Service Anchors

The strongest Wien MVP anchor is the City of Vienna page **Kostenlose Beratung und Unterstuetzung fuer Care Leaver**, which states a free offer for eligible Wien Care Leavers up to the 24th birthday and names common topics: housing, education, health, relationships/sexuality, finances, and authorities.

Other key source anchors:

- MA 11 Servicestelle for child/youth welfare routing.
- RIS / WKJHG 2013 § 33 for cautious legal background on "Hilfen fuer junge Erwachsene" in Wien.
- BMI and Stadt Wien emergency-number pages for crisis contacts.
- PSD Wien for acute psychiatric crisis in Wien.
- Rat auf Draht for youth advice.
- KIJA Wien for rights, complaints, and ombuds support.
- U25 Wiener Jugendunterstuetzung for work, education, and social-support routing for under-25 Wien users.
- MA 50 / Wohnbeihilfe and Wohnberatung Wien for housing-related appointment prep.
- FSW Wohnungslosenhilfe and Caritas JUCA for housing crisis routes, with careful non-guarantee wording.
- NEBA, AusBildung bis 18, waff, AMS-linked routes for work/education support.
- FMA Basiskonto, OBS, oesterreich.gv.at, and OeGK for money/admin/insurance claims.

## Existing Organizations And Complement Strategy

`careleaver.eu` should complement, not compete with, existing organizations.

Recommended position:

- Treat **Care Leaver Oesterreich / careleaver.at** as the primary lived-experience and self-representation voice.
- Treat the **City of Vienna Care Leaver counselling page** as the core Wien official route.
- Treat **KIJA Wien** as rights/complaint support, not as a benefit decision service.
- Treat **FICE Austria, DÖJ, Plattform Jugendhilfe 18+**, and similar networks as system and advocacy context, not first-click crisis help.
- Link to provider-specific offers such as SOS-Kinderdorf, Volkshilfe Wien, Pro Juventute, Diakonie de La Tour, Tirol, and Vorarlberg only after verifying the exact eligibility and regional access route.

Important naming risk: `careleaver.eu` is close to `careleaver.at`. The public site should say clearly that it is an independent information/navigation site unless an explicit collaboration is agreed.

## Product / UX Direction

Best low-maintenance features:

1. A top "Ich brauche Hilfe mit ..." router: Krise, Wohnen, Geld, Termin, Dokumente, Beschwerde/Rechte.
2. Wien appointment-prep kits for MA 11/Care Leaver counselling, U25/MA 40/AMS, Wohnbeihilfe/MA 50, KIJA, and housing crisis.
3. Visible source links and "last checked" dates for every high-risk section.
4. A static contact-list pattern that prefers official contact pages over copied phone/address details unless recently checked.
5. Rename the current "Anspruchs-Check" to a safer "Naechste-Schritte-Finder" or "Vorbereitungs-Check".
6. Print styles and section print buttons instead of separately maintained PDFs.
7. Short German scripts for calls, emails, and appointments.
8. Bundesland pages only one at a time after source logs exist.

Do not build yet:

- backend
- accounts
- user-submitted forms
- analytics/tracking
- chatbot
- crowdsourced contact database
- big redesign

## Risk And Safety Findings

Highest risks:

- The current "Anspruchs-Check" can sound like a benefit/legal decision tool.
- Legal and benefit claims can become harmful if worded as guarantees.
- Crisis numbers and service hours can become stale.
- Contact details copied into HTML are easy to age silently.
- localStorage can preserve sensitive appointment details on shared devices.
- Placeholder Impressum/contact fields reduce trust and may create compliance risk.
- The domain name may be confused with Care Leaver Oesterreich.

Mitigations:

- Use "kann moeglich sein", "pruefe mit", "frag bei", "nimm mit", not entitlement promises.
- Put "not legal advice / not therapy / not emergency service" near high-risk tools, not only in the footer.
- Use official links and "last checked" dates beside claims.
- Review crisis/contact/benefit claims every 3 months.
- Make local saving explicit and easy to delete; consider opt-in or session-only storage for sensitive fields.
- Resolve Impressum/contact placeholders before outreach.
- Add a clear independence/non-affiliation note unless collaboration is agreed.
