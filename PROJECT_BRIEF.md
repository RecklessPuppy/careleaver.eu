# Project Brief

Last updated: 2026-04-29

## Project Definition

`careleaver.eu` is a practical Austrian Care Leaver resource website that helps young people leaving child/youth welfare understand next steps, find support, prepare for appointments, and access trustworthy sources.

The site should be useful under real pressure: on a phone, before an appointment, during a crisis, or when preparing documents with a trusted adult, peer, social worker, mentor, or support person.

## Primary Audience

- Young people leaving or recently out of child/youth welfare in Austria.
- Especially young people around the transition to adulthood.
- First MVP focus: Wien.

## Secondary Audience

- Supportive adults, peers, social workers, youth workers, educators, mentors, foster families, and advocates.
- Future Codex chats and maintainers who need to update the site safely.

## Core User Needs

- "What should I do next?"
- "Who can I contact in Wien?"
- "What documents do I need?"
- "What rights or support options might matter?"
- "How do I prepare for an appointment?"
- "What should I do if things are urgent or unsafe?"

## Product Principles

- Practical over polished.
- Source-linked over confident-sounding.
- Austria-first, Wien-first.
- Static and durable unless there is a strong reason to add tooling.
- German public content unless otherwise requested.
- Mobile-friendly and print-friendly.
- No fake certainty. If something is not verified, say so or leave it out.

## Current Site Shape

The current public site is a single static German HTML page with:

- a crisis/emergency bar
- a 5-S checklist
- a transition timeline
- compact rights cards
- small browser-side tools
- a Wien contact list
- beta Bundesland placeholders
- a one-page action plan
- legal/privacy/disclaimer notes

Public URL: `https://careleaver.eu`

Custom domain is controlled by `CNAME` and should be preserved.

## Non-Goals For Now

- No big redesign yet.
- No backend.
- No account system.
- No form submissions.
- No collection of sensitive personal data.
- No claim that the website replaces legal advice, medical advice, therapy, or official counselling.

## Success For The MVP

The Austria/Wien MVP is successful when a young person or helper can:

- understand the main transition areas quickly
- identify the next one or two actions
- find source-linked Wien support contacts
- prepare a document checklist
- print or save useful appointment material
- see clear crisis routes without confusion
- understand when information was last checked
